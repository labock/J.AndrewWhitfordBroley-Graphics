CSCI 321—Graphics

Final Programming Project: due Friday, April 29, 2014

The final programming project is an opportunity to put together the various graphics programming elements that we've learned this semester. There is no fixed assignment, but there are a number of requirements that are discussed below.

Students who have submitted at least four of the first five assignments may choose to be part of a group project; those who have submitted fewer than four of the first five assignments must do an individual project. A group many have two or three students. I will expect a group project to be somewhat more involved than an individual project, but there is no firm metric on that expectation; in particular, group projects don't have to somehow be “twice as good” as individual projects.

Projects should present a complete graphical environment, using appropriate modeling, transformations, lighting and shading, and texture mapping; if needed, the project should also include use of Bézier curves and/or hierarchical modeling. The project must also include animation and/or the ability to move the viewer and/or light(s) and/or some of the objects in the scene with user controls.

Successful projects in the past have included:

  • Modeling a well-known building on one of the campuses
  • Modeling a chess-board with its pieces and the ability to move the pieces on the board
  • Modeling a race course with the viewer following an animation around the course
  • Modeling a person, robot, or animal walking across the ground
  • Modeling the Solar System, with texture maps on the surfaces of the planets
