CSCI 321

OVERVIEW—Description and Objectives

Computer graphics is a fascinating area of study in the larger discipline of computer science. In this course, we will survey programming techniques for producing three dimensional computer graphics. Topics will include modeling, geometric objects and transformations, viewing, shading, curves, animation, and the modern GPU pipeline.

Students who successfully complete this course will be able:

  • to create and display geometric objects in a computer window
  • to change viewpoints and projection models in a graphical image
  • to apply techniques like lighting, shading, texture mapping, and simple animation to graphical objects
  • to discuss some of the problems and issues involved in producing realistic computer graphics
